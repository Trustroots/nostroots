# nr-web Style Guide

This guide keeps `nr-web` clear for first-time users, especially people arriving from Trustroots.

## Product framing

- Explain purpose quickly: map-based traveler notes by area.
- Keep migration framing lightweight: Nostroots extends Trustroots into Nostr.
- Emphasize confidence cues: users can read first, then connect a key to post.

## Copy guidelines

- Use short, action-led sentences: what this is, why it matters, what to do next.
- Prefer friendly instructions over protocol-heavy wording.
- Mention Nostr terms (`nsec`, `npub`) only with immediate context.
- For warnings, include capability reassurance where possible ("you can still read notes").

## First-impression UX rules

- Header should communicate app purpose and first interaction path.
- First-run onboarding should answer:
  - Why key setup exists
  - What to import/generate
  - Why Trustroots username linking helps
- Empty states should always include at least one clear next step.
- One-time hints are preferred over persistent noise for newcomer guidance.

## Visual guidelines (lightweight)

- Keep existing aesthetic (minimal, map-forward), avoid heavy redesigns in routine updates.
- Use subtle helper text instead of large callouts by default.
- Preserve high contrast for guidance copy on overlays and headers.
- Maintain spacing that groups related actions (key import/generate, verification steps).

## Reusable phrases

- "Read traveler notes by area."
- "Click the map to explore."
- "You can read without a key."
- "Connect a key when you want to post."
- "Link your Trustroots identity so relays can verify your posts."
